Camelids

Camelids are unusual in that their modern distribution is almost the inverse of their area of origin.
Camelids first appeared very early in the evolution of the even-toed ungulates.
Among the earliest camelids was the rabbit-sized Protylopus, which still had four toes on each foot.
camelids such as Poebrotherium had lost the two lateral toes, and were about the size of a modern goat.

Lions

The lion is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera.
It has a muscular, deep-chested body, short, rounded head, round ears,
and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic;
adult male lions have a prominent mane.
